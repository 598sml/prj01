# Project Topic Discussion Plan

Goal: to vet your proposed project to with your peers

The underlying goal is to help you refine. At this point your project does not need to be finalized. You should, however, have a promising direction.

You will receive feedback from your group and help your peers do the same.

### 1. Prepare your mini-presentation (10–15 minutes)

The first step is to prepare a short description of your project.

Your description to your peers should be about 2–3 minutes long. A single slide or a outline is fine. Focus on clarity.

* what is the problem
* what approach do you plan to take
* how you will evaluate the result
* reflection and stretch goals

### 2. Small-group project presentations

Bbreak into small groups and present the project.

**Step 1** — Presentation (2–3 minutes)
Explain the project

**Step 2** — written feedback (2 minutes)
Each group member fills out the peer feedback form.

Complete the form individually and on your own before discussion so that
everyone has time to think and so that the presenter receives written feedback.

**Step 3** — Group discussion (3–4 minutes)

The goal is to help the presenter clarify the project and to refine the scope.

### 3. Identify the key unresolved issue

Identify the single biggest unresolved issue for the project.
